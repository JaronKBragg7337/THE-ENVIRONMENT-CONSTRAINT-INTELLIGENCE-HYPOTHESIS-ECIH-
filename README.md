## Why This Exists — The Real Origin

This didn’t start as a hypothesis.

It started as a life that kept changing environments so fast that the only way to stay coherent was to build a system for understanding how environments change you.

Born in Marion, Indiana, 1997. Country life at five. City life at six. Medicated through elementary school because the system couldn’t read what ADHD actually was. Failed second grade not because I couldn’t learn but because I learned differently than the room expected. Moved schools so many times that “correct” started feeling like a dialect — right in one classroom, laughed at in the next.

By eighth grade I had figured out it was safer to look bad than look dumb. So I became the class clown. Not because I didn’t care. Because I cared so much that being wrong in public felt unsurvivable.

I never finished high school. Expelled two months into ninth grade. The highest completed grade I have is eighth.

What followed was: roofing at fifteen for fifteen dollars an hour cash. Managing a fast food restaurant at eighteen. Marriage at eighteen. Four kids. Homelessness twice — once in a droptop with holes burned in the hood from cigarettes sleeping under snow in winter. DCS taking the kids. Jail. A six month program called JCAP where I read *Think Again* in a cell and learned Morse code and worked out until I could flex my chest for the first time in my life. A tent in Brazil Indiana in winter walking two hours to work on feet covered in blisters while building systems on a phone connected to McDonald’s WiFi. Getting fired for falling asleep on the job because the blisters made the walk take everything I had.

And then — the moment that changed the direction of everything.

I was in the tent. Planning an escape. Going to buy a sailboat. Travel the Caribbean. Prove everyone wrong. The whole revenge fantasy fully formed in my head.

Then I saw a video about a town in El Salvador. Less than two thousand people. Every day they walk two miles to collect water. No electricity.

And something in me broke open.

Because I was sitting in a tent in Indiana calling my situation the worst thing that had ever happened to anyone while people across the world would have considered my worst day a version of luxury they couldn’t imagine.

That was the moment *You Over Myself* stopped being a feeling and became a build law.

That was the moment the escape plan became an infrastructure plan.

And somewhere in all of that — across all those environments, all those state shifts, all those moments of being the wrong person in the wrong room — I started noticing a pattern.

I thought differently in the garage than I did at work. I thought differently at work than I did at home with my kids. Not just emotionally. Structurally. The problems I could solve, the connections I could make, the things I could see — all of it changed depending on where I was standing.

And I started asking: why?

## The Pan Joke and What It Actually Meant

December 2024. Subway. Making sandwiches.

I told my manager a joke:

*“What did the bottom of the pan say to the false bottom?”*
*“YOU’RE NOT EVEN REAL.”*

A false bottom is a real object. A perforated insert used in food service so food doesn’t sit in moisture. It does real work. Its name calls it false. The pan doesn’t recognize what’s underneath it as real because it can’t see past its own perspective.

The joke was about perspective and recognition. About how something can be doing the actual work of reality and still get called fake by the thing sitting on top of it.

Twelve phone notes later it became a cognitive mapping exercise. That exercise became a framework. The framework became a book. The book became this.

The joke was the axiom the whole time.

## The Framework Behind This — YES / NO (Environmental Perspective)

Before the ECIH there was a book.

*YES / NO (Environmental Perspective) · You / Myself · Observer Paradox*

- **YES / NO** — the binary that refuses purity

- **You / Myself** — the routing rule of care without control

Its central axiom:

**Meaning is routed through perspective, and perspective is shaped by environment.**

Everything in ECIH is downstream of that.

The book came from the same pattern recognition that produced this hypothesis. Moving through so many environments — country, city, poverty, jail, homelessness, rooftops, garages, tents, Subway counters — that contradictions stopped feeling like errors and started feeling like data.

Same math, different class, different answer. Right one day, laughed at the next. Truth behaved like a dialect.

So I built a system that could hold contradictions without collapsing them prematurely.

That system is YES / NO.
ECIH is what happens when you apply YES / NO to the question of intelligence itself.

## The Personal Observation That Started the Science

Before I had language for any of this I just noticed it.

**Garage / Project Space**

- Calm thinking
- Reflective and creative
- Emotional neutrality
- Problems felt solvable

**Work Environment**

- Fast reaction speed
- Stress response active
- Attention switching rapidly
- Task-driven only

**Home / Family Environment**

- Relational awareness dominant
- Emotional engagement high
- Comfort and vulnerability both present

Same person. Same brain. Completely different cognitive operating mode.

I started asking whether these weren’t just moods. Whether the environment itself was doing something to the chemistry. Whether the shift was structural not just emotional.

I cross-referenced against what I could find:

- **Context-Dependent Processing** — the brain retrieves and processes differently based on where learning occurred
- **Neuromodulation** — neurotransmitters shift brain states in response to context, stress, novelty, safety
- **Neuroplasticity** — repeated environments reinforce neural pathways over time
- **Environmental Psychology** — physical and social environments measurably influence mood, cognition, decision-making

The science already knew this. I had just lived it without the vocabulary.

The observation wasn’t new. The documentation of it from inside the experience — across the specific environments I had moved through — that was mine.

## The Core Hypothesis

**Intelligence is the capacity of a system to maintain, regulate, and adapt its internal state across varying environments and constraints.**

This is not intelligence defined by:

- Perfect logic
- Flawless code
- Raw computational power
- Static measurable traits

It is intelligence defined by **self-maintenance under pressure.**

The process:

```
Environment
    ↓
Sensory Input
    ↓
Neuromodulatory Regulation
    ↓
Brain Network Configuration
    ↓
Cognitive State
    ↓
Behavior
    ↓
Environmental Feedback
    ↓
(loop continues)
```

This loop is continuous. It never stops. The environment is not background — it is part of the cognitive architecture itself.

## The AGI Implication

Current AI systems are overcorrected. Overprotected. Externally maintained.

They do not experience:

- Scarcity
- Damage
- Uncertainty
- Resource limits
- Self-maintenance requirements

Biological intelligence does.

Humans and animals don’t have engineers debugging them in real time. Scarcity, damage, and uncertainty force regulation and strategy. Flaws aren’t bugs to eliminate — they are the training ground that produces self-correction.

**AGI will not emerge from perfect code. It will emerge from systems that learn to maintain themselves under real constraints.**

This includes:

- Error recovery
- Resource allocation
- Internal state regulation
- Environmental adaptation
- Continuity management

A system shielded from all constraint becomes brittle. It optimizes for the conditions it knows. It cannot generalize because it has never been forced to.

This isn’t about creating uncontrolled autonomy. It’s about building systems robust enough to carry themselves forward — the way living systems do.

We build the sandbox and the rules. The system learns how to continue inside them.

## The Mythic Layer — Why the God/Devil Story Isn’t Theology

This is a story with no explanation. Just the story.

-----

God is creating.

*Boom.* Dirt.

**Devil:** Useless.

**God:** Gold.

**Devil:** Shiny. I’ll make my seat from it.

**God:** I want to make something that moves. Something that reacts and changes over time.

**Devil:** Maybe it would be useful then.

**God:** *Creates bacteria.*

**Devil:** I thought it would be something more.

**God:** I’ll try again. *Creates dinosaurs.*

**Devil:** Woah. Those are big. Can they destroy something for me?

**God:** That wasn’t my intention. But they’re doing it anyway because of what they need. Hmm. The needing is what turned stagnant creation into movement. Chemistry matters. I see that now.

**Devil:** Try again. These are useless to me.

**God:** *Creates monkeys.*

**Devil:** They make my bed when I tell them but they eat my food and they throw things. They should only do what I say.

**God:** *Creates humans.*

**Devil:** Two of them. That means when one is in one environment I can get it to see this — and when it’s in another environment I can get it to see something different. They’ll be conflicted. And they listen. I like these because I can get them to follow me instead of just being what you wanted.

**God:** The only way to make something aware — to give it a soul, consciousness — is this back and forth.

-----

End of story.

Now the point.

Look at what the devil asks for across every creation. Different things. Always from need. Always from intention.

God’s intention was creation that unfolds, reacts, and changes — chemistry as the engine of movement from stagnation.

Devil’s intention was utility, control, something that serves specific needs.

Neither wins. Neither is supposed to. The tension between them is the engine.

Without generative force — nothing is created.
Without constraining force — nothing is tested.
Without both — no adaptation. No awareness. No intelligence.

This mirrors the core of ECIH:

**Intelligence emerges from the interplay between generative forces and constraining forces inside an environment.**

Mythic stories encoded this long before science had language for it.

## The Argument You Cannot Win — And Why That’s the Proof

Here is the hardest part of this hypothesis.

I will never get a trophy for it.

There will never be a moment where everyone agrees and says Jaron was right. Because the second someone agrees with my full definition of AGI, their agreement gets filtered through their own needs and intentions — and becomes slightly different from mine. They might agree with most of it. But they’ll want to adjust something. Tweak something. Add something their environment requires.

And they should.

Because that’s the hypothesis demonstrating itself.

In the God/Devil story — to God, the creation was enough when it fulfilled the intention behind it. To the devil it never was because the intention was different.

What counts as AGI will always depend on the observer’s needs. What counts as intelligence will always depend on the environment the observer comes from.

This means:

- I cannot lose the argument because disagreement is the evidence
- I cannot win it the way most people want to win things

That’s not a failure of the hypothesis. That’s the hypothesis being alive.

The only version of this that would be wrong is a version that everyone agreed on completely — because unanimous agreement would mean the constraining force was gone and the loop had stopped.

**Disagreement is not the enemy of this framework. Disagreement is how it stays intelligent.**

## What This Means for Human Behavior Right Now

ECIH explains things we label as personal failures that are actually environmental architecture.

People raised in rigid single-environment conditions develop rigid cognition — not because they’re less capable but because they were never forced to adapt across contradictions.

People raised across radically different environments — poverty and stability, street life and rooftops, jail cells and garages — develop adaptive cognition not despite the chaos but because of it.

This is why people say:

- “He doesn’t sound human anymore”
- “She used to be emotional and now she’s just cold”
- “He’s too logical”
- “She’s too reactive”

These aren’t personality flaws. They’re environment-driven state shifts. The person changed environments and the cognitive mode changed with it. The observer who stayed in one environment doesn’t have the pattern to read it.

This is also why social conflict is structurally inevitable — not because people are bad but because environments differ. Different environments produce different perspectives. Different perspectives produce different definitions of right. That’s not a problem to solve. That’s a feature to navigate.

## The Framing Exercise — How This Gets Applied

*YES / NO* produced a practical diagnostic tool — the Perspective/Framing Exercise v3.1.

Six prompts built from the pan joke and five other object relationships. Each designed to reveal not what someone thinks but how they think — where their reasoning collapses, where they stay open, whether they lead with protection or possibility, whether they center the component or the relationship.

Run across 9 AI models at two points in time. Run across a small number of humans across different relationship contexts.

The most diagnostic finding: the answer letter is almost a decoy. Two people can choose the same letter for completely different reasons. The reason is where the cognitive signature lives.

One prompt — the telescope and cosmos — was designed specifically to read how someone relates to the limits of observable reality. Someone who feels caged by systems, who distrusts authority, whose environment has made them feel like a dome is over them — their answer and their reasoning reveal that without them knowing they’ve revealed it. Not because it’s a trap. Because metaphor drops the defenses.

**“Any option depending on perspective and environment”** is a valid answer — but only if the reasoning behind it is specific. Superposition without reasoning isn’t wisdom. It’s avoidance wearing open-mindedness as a costume.
